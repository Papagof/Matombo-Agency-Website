Most restaurants treat Instagram like a scrapbook: a nice plate here, a busy Friday there. It looks good, but it doesn't move the one number that matters — **reservations**.

The venues that win on social treat their feed like a funnel. Here's how to build one.

## Start with the crave, not the camera

People don't book a table because your feed is pretty. They book because a single piece of content triggered a craving they couldn't shake. Your job is to manufacture that craving on purpose.

- Shoot the **hero moments**: the cheese pull, the tableside pour, the dessert that arrives on fire.
- Capture **atmosphere**, not just food: the golden-hour light, the full room, the laughter.
- Show the **people**: your chef plating, your bartender shaking, regulars being greeted by name.

## Post for the algorithm, sell in the caption

Reels are still the fastest way to reach people who've never heard of you. But reach is worthless if it doesn't convert.

> Every viral video should have a quiet, confident call to action: "Tuesdays book out first — link in bio to reserve."

Make the path from crave to reservation as short as humanly possible. A "Reserve" button in your bio and story stickers on every relevant post are the minimum.

## Use paid social to fill the quiet nights

Organic reach is a lottery. Paid social is a dial you can turn. We run tightly geo-targeted campaigns for restaurants that:

1. Target a small radius around the venue — the people who can actually walk in.
2. Push a specific occasion: date night, the new tasting menu, bottomless brunch.
3. Send straight to the reservation page, and measure success in **booked covers**, not clicks.

A modest weekly budget aimed at midweek slots often pays for itself in a single seating.

## Turn diners into your content team

Your guests are already filming their meals. Make it effortless — and rewarding — for them to tag you.

- A signature, photogenic dish designed to be shared.
- A subtle, well-lit "moment" in the space people naturally photograph.
- A reason to tag: a monthly repost, a drink on the house for the best shot.

User-generated content is trusted more than anything you'll ever post yourself.

## Measure what matters

Vanity metrics are a trap. Track the numbers tied to revenue:

- Reservations attributed to social and paid campaigns.
- Cost per booked cover.
- Repeat visits from your most-engaged followers.

---

Instagram isn't a highlight reel — it's your busiest host, working 24/7. Give it a clear job, point it at your reservation page, and watch the quiet nights disappear.
