If you run a shortlet — or a small portfolio of them — the platforms have probably been very good to you. They filled your calendar when you had no audience of your own. But that convenience comes at a cost: commissions, service fees, and payment charges that can quietly add up to 15–18% of everything you earn.

You don't have to leave the platforms. You just need somewhere for your best guests to book directly. Here's how independent owners build that channel.

## Give your portfolio a brand, not just listings

A collection of platform listings is a commodity. A **brand** is something guests remember, search for, and return to.

- A name and identity that spans all your units.
- Consistent, styled photography that feels like one collection.
- A story: who you are, what a stay with you feels like, why you're different.

The moment your guests think of you as "Villa Serai" rather than "that Airbnb," direct booking becomes possible.

## Build a booking site that guests actually trust

Guests hesitate to book direct because they worry it's less safe than the platform. Remove every reason for that doubt:

1. A fast, professional website with real photography and clear pricing.
2. A proper booking engine with instant confirmation and secure payment.
3. Reviews, policies, and contact details displayed openly.

> Trust is the entire game. If booking direct feels even slightly risky, guests will default to the platform — and you'll pay for it.

## Capture guests you already paid to acquire

Every platform booking is a guest you've *already* paid a commission to reach. Capture them properly:

- Collect their email at (or after) check-in, with permission.
- Send a warm post-stay message with a direct-booking incentive.
- Build a simple win-back sequence for their next trip to your city.

The second stay should cost you nothing in commission.

## Make direct the better deal — visibly

Give guests a real, stated reason to skip the platform next time:

- A direct-booking discount that reflects the fees you're saving.
- A small perk: late checkout, a local welcome basket, a bottle on arrival.
- Flexible terms you control, rather than the platform's.

## Drive your own demand

Once the direct channel exists, feed it. Even a modest, well-targeted paid social budget — pointed at your own booking site and measured against real reservations — can shift a meaningful share of bookings off the platforms and onto you.

---

The platforms are a fine place to *meet* guests. They're an expensive place to keep them. Build a brand, a trustworthy site, and a habit of capturing guests directly, and that 18% starts flowing back where it belongs — to you.
