The developers who sell out fastest don't wait for the show home. They build a waitlist of qualified, emotionally-committed buyers while the site is still a hoarding and a render.

Pre-launch marketing isn't about shouting "for sale." It's about manufacturing desire and scarcity in the right order. Here's how.

## Sell the story before the square footage

Buyers don't fall in love with a floor plan. They fall in love with a vision of their life in a place. Before a single price is published, your marketing should establish:

- The **neighbourhood narrative**: what it feels like to live here.
- The **design point of view**: the materials, the light, the details that set it apart.
- The **lifestyle**: mornings, evenings, weekends — rendered vividly.

## Build the waitlist, then guard the gate

A waitlist does two things at once: it captures demand *and* it manufactures exclusivity. Done well, it turns buyers into insiders.

1. Launch a simple, beautiful landing page with a single job: register interest.
2. Offer genuine early-access value — first pick of units, founder pricing, a private preview.
3. Qualify as they register: budget, timeline, unit preference. Now your sales team knows exactly who to call.

> Scarcity you can prove — "only 40 units, 900 on the waitlist" — is the most powerful line in a launch.

## Run high-intent paid search for real buyers

Someone searching "2-bedroom apartments [your district] new development" is not browsing — they're buying. We build tightly-themed search campaigns that:

- Capture buyers at the exact moment of intent.
- Route them to a unit-matched landing page, not a generic homepage.
- Feed a CRM so no lead ever goes cold.

## Nurture relentlessly between registration and release

Most developments lose buyers in the silence between "I'm interested" and "units are available." A structured nurture sequence keeps the vision warm:

- Construction progress updates that build anticipation.
- Design and neighbourhood deep-dives that reinforce the story.
- Countdown moments as the release date approaches.

## Orchestrate the launch as an event

When you finally open, it should feel like a moment, not a memo. Coordinate the waitlist release, a private preview, and your paid campaigns to hit within the same window — so demand arrives all at once and scarcity does the closing for you.

---

Concrete takes months to pour. Demand takes just as long to build — so start now. Get the story, the waitlist, and the nurture working together, and it's entirely possible to be sold out before the show home ever opens its doors.
