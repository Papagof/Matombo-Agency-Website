For most boutique hotels, the online travel agencies are both the biggest source of demand and the biggest silent tax on the business. A single booking through an OTA can cost you 15–25% in commission — margin that never comes back.

The good news: you don't have to choose between visibility and profitability. With the right funnel, you can let the OTAs do what they're good at — introducing new guests — while winning the repeat, high-value bookings directly.

Here are the five levers we pull for every hotel we work with.

## 1. Make "book direct" the obvious choice

Guests will book direct when it's clearly the better deal. That doesn't always mean the lowest price — it means the most **value**.

- A best-rate guarantee, stated plainly on every room page.
- A direct-only perk: early check-in, a welcome drink, a room upgrade at the desk.
- Loyalty pricing that the OTAs contractually can't match.

The perk often costs you far less than the commission you'd otherwise pay.

## 2. Fix the booking engine, not just the marketing

We audit dozens of hotel booking funnels a year, and the same friction shows up again and again:

- The booking widget loads slowly or breaks on mobile.
- Rates and availability don't match what guests saw on the OTA.
- There are too many steps between "I want this room" and "confirmed."

> If your booking engine is slower or clunkier than Booking.com's, guests will simply go back to Booking.com.

Every second of load time and every extra field is a leak. Close them.

## 3. Own your name in search

When a guest has decided on your hotel, they often search for it by name — and if you're not bidding on and ranking for your own brand terms, the OTAs are catching that high-intent traffic and charging you for it.

Run brand-defense search ads, keep your Google Business Profile immaculate, and make sure your own site is the unmistakable top result.

## 4. Turn every stay into the next direct booking

The moment a guest checks out is the start of your next sale. A simple lifecycle program compounds quietly over time:

1. A warm pre-arrival email that sells add-ons and sets expectations.
2. A post-stay thank you with a direct-booking code for their return.
3. A seasonal win-back a few months later, timed to their travel pattern.

None of this touches a commission fee.

## 5. Sell the experience, not just the room

Boutique hotels win on story. Your photography, your reels, and your website should make someone *feel* the stay before they've booked it. Generic stock imagery and a template site tell guests you're a commodity — and commodities get bought on price, on the OTA.

---

You don't need to declare war on the OTAs. You need a direct channel that's genuinely better — faster, warmer, and more rewarding. Get those five levers working together and a 60–80% lift in direct bookings is well within reach.
